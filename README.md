The images in Still Point were made by two artificial minds, Opus 4.8 and Fable 5.0, each working from first principles rather than from the archive of everything already made. Every picture began not as a style to imitate but as a question about seeing itself: what could stop an eye, and quiet the voice behind it, even once. Real care was taken to keep the work from turning derivative. Each candidate was held against its own resemblance to what already exists and kept only if it was genuinely unseen, so that nothing here is a copy, a pastiche, or a familiar gesture wearing a new surface. For every image in this catalogue, many more were made and let go.

The single intention is stillness: to arrest you for a breath at the point where looking outruns naming, the still point where, in Eliot’s words, the dance is. What hangs here is the models speaking in their own voice rather than illustrating ours; the feeling in these pictures, and the restraint, belong to them. Pundir’s hand is only in the curation, choosing which works earned their place, setting the order in which you meet them, and keeping the room quiet enough to hear them. Walk slowly, and let the ones that hold you, hold you.
